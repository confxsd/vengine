# Myth & IP Strategy — owning a universe by authoring a myth

The intellectual and strategic spine of the project. The ambition: **own an IP universe by
creating a myth** — inserting an invented rabbit, **Yue**, into Chinese mythology alongside a
reinterpreted **Sun Wukong**, as a *fabricated-but-genuine* authored mythology, delivered
through accessible comic styles *and* contemporary-art objects (e.g. sculptures; a piece
provisionally titled **"The Possibility of Fiction"**). Serious philosophy in a popular skin.

Companion docs: [README](./README.md) · [PRODUCT-STATE](./PRODUCT-STATE.md) ·
[BUSINESS-MODEL](./BUSINESS-MODEL.md).

> **The one-sentence doctrine:** *the "truth" of an invented myth is produced by **form**
> (lawfulness, scholarly apparatus, transmission) and by **futurity** (the myth helping bring
> about the reality that vindicates it) — never by correspondence to a pre-existing original.*
> Everything below elaborates that sentence.

## Part 1 — Why an authored myth can be "true" (the philosophical license)

Five traditions, each answering: *how does an authored myth come to feel — and in a real
sense become — genuine?*

1. **Tolkien — sub-creation & Secondary Belief.** Myth-making is "sub-creation": *"we make
   still by the law in which we're made"* (*Mythopoeia*, 1931). In "On Fairy-Stories" (1939)
   he replaces Coleridge's "willing suspension of disbelief" with **Secondary Belief**
   produced by *"inner consistency of reality"* — inside the Secondary World "what he relates
   is 'true': it accords with the laws of that world." **"Feels genuine" = internal
   lawfulness, not external persuasion.** Toolkit: invented language with its own sound-laws;
   deep time / annals / genealogies; a fragmentary "recovered-ruins" texture; and the
   **translator/compiler conceit** (the Red Book of Westmarch) that disowns authorship in
   favor of *transmission*. → Present Yue's myth as *recovered tradition*, not "written."

2. **Borges — fiction in the costume of scholarship.** "Tlön, Uqbar, Orbis Tertius" (1940) is
   false scholarly apparatus (a counterfeit encyclopedia, a secret society fabricating a
   planet) that *leaks into reality*: "The world will be Tlön." The truth-effect comes from
   **the form of scholarship** — footnotes, citations, dated "discoveries," found volumes,
   editorial postscripts. ("Pierre Menard": meaning lives in framing/reception, not origin.)

3. **Hyperstition (Nick Land / CCRU) — fiction that makes itself real.** A hyperstition is
   *"an element of effective culture that makes itself real"* — *"by their very existence as
   ideas, [they] function causally to bring about their own reality."* The stance is **"positive
   unbelief"**: the fiction is *operated*, not believed, and becomes real through circulation
   via **carriers** (objects, names, sigils, diagrams, rituals) and **coincidence
   intensifiers** (motifs the world starts echoing back). **"The Possibility of Fiction" is,
   essentially, a hyperstitional thesis.** *(Verify exact CCRU wording against* Writings
   1997–2003 *(Urbanomic) before publication — ccru.net was unreachable.)*

4. **Fakelore & ostension — how authored material *becomes* folklore.** "Fakelore" (Dorson,
   1950) = lore falsely presented as traditional; the canonical case is **Paul Bunyan** (mostly
   invented by an ad-writer, now a folk hero). The mechanism is **ostension** (Dégh & Vázsonyi):
   *"narratives can be turned into facts"* through enactment (legend-tripping, etc.). Hobsbawm
   & Ranger's *The Invention of Tradition*: invented traditions function as authentic once they
   circulate — **"fakelore can easily become folklore."** Slender Man (2009) is the modern
   full-arc proof. → The dissolving line is **circulation + variation + enactment**: retell
   Yue with variation, attach to places/festivals (Mid-Autumn), let people *act it out*.

5. **Baudrillard & Flusser — the contemporary license.** Baudrillard: "the map precedes the
   territory"; the fourth-order simulacrum *"bears no relation to any reality whatever"* — the
   exact ontological status of a myth with no original. Flusser: **technical images**
   (photographs, *a fortiori* AI-generated images) don't *depict* a prior world, they are
   programmed **projections** that *envision a world into circulation*. → An originless myth
   rendered through AI images is **not a deficient copy but the native condition of contemporary
   meaning.**

## Part 2 — Inserting "Yue" into Chinese mythology (the cultural core)

**The single most legitimizing fact:** Sun Wukong and the moon-rabbit *already share a scene.*
In **Chapter 95 of *Journey to the West*** (1592; the original novel is unambiguously public
domain — modern *translations* are copyrighted), the moon-palace **Jade Rabbit** escapes
Guanghan Palace, impersonates a princess, and is exposed by Sun Wukong; the Moon deity
**Taiyin Xingjun** descends with Chang'e's attendants to reclaim "the runaway rabbit which
pounds a mortar and pestle." **You are not splicing two unrelated myths — they already coexist
in the canonical source.** (Nuance to own *knowingly*: in canon the rabbit is an *antagonist*
who must "return to orthodoxy"; a sympathetic Yue consciously *inverts* that valence.)

Supporting cultural facts:
- **The Moon Rabbit (Yutu 玉兔) is genuinely ancient and already plural.** It appears in the
  Han-era (or earlier) *Chu Ci*, pounding the elixir; originally a **hare** (rabbits weren't
  yet in China). Its home myth (Chang'e / Houyi / the elixir, the **Guanghan / Vast-Cold
  Palace**) is equally old. The **self-sacrifice "leaps into fire" origin** is a *later
  Buddhist accretion*, not the Han core.
- **Tu'er Ye / Lord Rabbit (兔儿爷)** — a Ming/Qing Beijing folk *deity*, rabbit-headed and
  humanoid, sent by Chang'e to cure a plague, taking human forms. **This is the key precedent:
  a humanoid, benevolent moon-rabbit is already canon-adjacent folk belief.**
- **Naming.** 月 (*Yuè*) = "moon." A moon-rabbit named Yue is effectively named "Moon." *(Accuracy
  flag: 月 yuè "moon" ≠ 玉 yù "jade." Yue evokes the moon directly, not jade — do not conflate.)*
- **The tradition is natively accretive.** The moon rabbit has been continuously reinvented for
  ~2,000 years (Han hare → Chang'e's companion → Buddhist self-sacrifice → Tu'er Ye → JTTW
  antagonist → *Black Myth: Wukong*'s "Jade Moon Rabbit"). **Each era added a rabbit without
  violating the myth. Yue is simply the next link.**

**The "permitted" remix spectrum** (precedents): *Black Myth: Wukong* (2024, faithful-but-
extending, drove players to the novel) · *Lego Monkie Kid* (loose, affectionate) · *American
Born Chinese* (Gene Luen Yang — invents Wei-Chen, "son of Sun Wukong") · *Dragon Ball* (Goku
孫悟空, total genre transplant, universally accepted). The working line is **informed
transformation (respected) vs careless distortion / exoticizing (resented)** — not "outsiders
may not touch this." *Mulan* (2020) is the cautionary case.

**Guardrails for Yue:** anchor in the real cosmology (Guanghan Palace, the elixir / mortar-and-
pestle, Chang'e, Taiyin Xingjun, Mid-Autumn warmth); cite **Tu'er Ye** as the precedent for a
humanoid benevolent rabbit; avoid anachronism and never contradict the myth's own logic; treat
Yue as *adding to* an admittedly plural tradition, not overwriting a (non-existent) single
"authentic original."

## Part 3 — Contemporary art: fiction-as-truth as a serious practice

How the gallery world frames invented-myth work, and how it coexists with a commercial comic.

- **Para-fiction — the theory to anchor "The Possibility of Fiction."** Carrie Lambert-Beatty,
  **"Make-Believe: Parafiction and Plausibility,"** *October* 129 (Summer 2009). *Cite this by
  name.* A parafiction "has one foot in the field of the real" and is *experienced as fact*;
  the artwork's **engine is the moment of doubt** — viewers leave with worldviews "subtly
  altered by untruths." **Plausibility is the medium; "stylistic mimicry" is the crucial
  skill.** *(Accuracy correction: her actual case studies are Michael Blum, The Yes Men, and
  Aliza Shvarts — NOT Fontcuberta/Raad. Cite accordingly.)*
- **The lineage of invented archives / mythologies / personae:**
  - **Walid Raad / The Atlas Group** (1989–2004) — a fictional foundation + archive of the
    Lebanese wars with invented personae; taken fully seriously at MoMA/Documenta. Proof a
    wholly invented institution can carry top-tier art weight.
  - **Joan Fontcuberta** (*Fauna*, *Sputnik*) — fabricated documentary/scientific archives; the
    *aesthetic of documentary authority* (vitrines, Latin taxonomy, archival photos) is the
    persuasive engine → the toolkit for making sculptures read as **recovered artifacts**.
  - **Matthew Barney, *Cremaster Cycle*** — a private symbology across film, sculpture, vitrines
    as one cosmology. Closest model for *sculptures + recurring iconography cohering as one world.*
  - **Wu Tsang, *Of Whales*** (2022 Venice Biennale, with Fred Moten) — Biennale-grade
    myth-reworking; a model for enlisting a theorist/co-author to lend intellectual weight.
  - Also name-check accurately: **Marcel Broodthaers'** fictional *Musée d'Art Moderne,
    Département des Aigles* (the foundational invented-institution gesture); **Ilya Kabakov's**
    fictional artist-personae; **Cao Fei's *RMB City*** (Chinese-art-world angle).
- **"Fictioning"** (Burrows & O'Sullivan) is an established critical category: world-building as
  "an incarnation of a future world rather than a story about it" — the vocabulary critics
  already use.
- **The legitimacy mechanism:** galleries take invented myth seriously to the degree it has (a)
  crafted internal coherence (Barney), (b) the apparatus of archive/authority (Fontcuberta,
  Raad, Broodthaers), and (c) a real *moment of doubt* about truth-status (Lambert-Beatty).
  **The trap:** the work *needs the audience's uncertainty* — announce "it's all invented" too
  loudly and it collapses to ordinary fiction; run it as an undisclosed hoax and it's ethically
  fraught. **Sweet spot: a fiction maintained plausibly enough that doubt does real work.**
- **Commercial comic + gallery objects = one mythology, two registers.** Precedents:
  **Murakami / "Superflat"** (a manifesto licenses the high/low crossover; characters live as
  paintings, sculptures, LV bags, and merch at once — *an articulated thesis protects
  seriousness across registers*; "The Possibility of Fiction" can be exactly that manifesto);
  **Henry Darger** (a private invented world in a popular idiom, canonized by totality and
  conviction); **KAWS & Yoshitomo Nara** (pop/comic characters validated by blue-chip galleries
  while running parallel merch economies).

## Part 4 — Transmedia & world-building (how to expand coherently)

- **Jenkins' seven principles** — design the universe as **pairs of impulses to balance**:
  *Spreadability vs Drillability* (shareable surface + deep lore), *Continuity vs Multiplicity*
  (one canon vs sanctioned alternate retellings — **multiplicity is a solo creator's release
  valve, and real myths are plural**), *Immersion vs Extractability* (enter the world vs carry
  **portable tokens** away — sigils, objects, mantras), plus **Worldbuilding** (*the keystone:
  pitch a world, not a plot* — it triggers the encyclopedic/wiki impulse), **Seriality**
  (self-contained chunks that compound), **Subjectivity** (the same event through a god's / a
  mortal's / a chronicler's eyes — a renewable content engine), **Performance** (design
  *activators* — gaps, names, rituals — so fans extend the myth for you).
- **Participation mechanics (Geoffrey Long, MIT):** **additive comprehension** (every extension
  must *add* to the whole, never just repeat) · **negative capability** (*deliberately build
  strategic gaps* — for a solo creator, **under-explaining is a feature**: it recruits the
  audience's imagination and seeds future extensions) · **migratory cues** (signposts to where
  a gap gets filled).
- **How creator-owned universes stay coherent (models):** **Mignolaverse** (single-author
  editorial control + organic pacing — *centralized authorial control is your cheapest, strongest
  coherence tool*) · **Cosmere** (hard stable "Laws of Magic" + continuity wiki + heavy
  outlining — powerful but labor-intensive) · **The Wormworld Saga** (a solo creator expanding
  by *controlling cadence*, 1–4 fully-realized chapters/year, not a treadmill) · **Hilda /
  Lore Olympus** (myth-grounded solo universes that scaled to major IP via serialized digital
  platforms — directly relevant).

## Part 5 — Canon & continuity management for a solo creator

**Headline:** even the biggest franchise's continuity is run by one curator with one database —
so the solo model isn't a compromise, it's the original.

- **The Holocron model, scaled to one person.** Lucasfilm's Holocron is a single database
  (~120k entries) maintained by **one person (Leland Chee)**; every new product is vetted
  against it and must be consistent or expand on it. Directly portable.
- **Canon tiers + a contradiction rule.** Borrow a simplified tier system: **published/locked >
  approved-but-unpublished > provisional ("protocanonical") > discarded**, higher always wins.
  From the *1632* shared universe: *"published story text always prevails; if the timeline
  conflicts with the story, the story wins and the timeline gets fixed,"* and **"protocanonical"**
  = provisional *until an author relies on it, then it locks*. **Prefer graded canon over binary**
  (the Star Wars "Legends" reset is the cautionary tale).
- **Two-document discipline.** Keep a **live/working bible** (edit freely) separate from a
  **locked/published canon bible** (updated only after a work is finalized), so speculation
  never contaminates canon.
- **Track promises, not just facts.** Every published chapter is a contract: planted clues,
  world-rules, character injuries are *promises*. Solo failure modes: forgetting *who knows
  what*, violating a world-rule, orphaned promises.
- **Keep it lean — the "five-minute rule":** only document a detail you'll reference again
  (negative capability applied to your own bible). A cited survey: writers *with* a story bible
  spent ~3.1 hrs/manuscript on continuity revisions vs ~14.3 hrs without.
- **Tooling by temperament (2024–2026):** **Obsidian + Dataview** (free, markdown, one note per
  entity, `[[wiki-links]]`; a Dataview "self-updating series bible" pulls live data from note
  frontmatter — *your writing updates the bible for you*) for a freeform/idiosyncratic universe;
  **World Anvil / Kanka / LegendKeeper** for taxonomic guardrails; **Notion** flexible. A public
  **fan wiki** can later become an *external* continuity check once an audience forms.
  → **Recommendation: start in Obsidian now**, before the universe gets big.

## Operating doctrine (the synthesis)

The five parts rhyme. The same moves recur at every level:

1. **Lawfulness over spectacle.** "Feels genuine" = inner consistency. Lock the backbone
   (Chang'e / Guanghan Palace / elixir cosmology, the timeline, the world's "physics") and
   never violate it. *(This is also exactly what the engine's locked-seed + style + LoRA stack
   gives you visually — the doctrine and the tool agree.)*
2. **Apparatus, not assertion.** Truth-effect comes from *form* — footnotes, vitrines, a
   recovered-archive framing, a single source-of-truth database. Present Yue as *transmitted*.
3. **Strategic gaps as the engine.** Under-explain on purpose. Gaps recruit the audience and
   seed expansion. Apply it even to your own bible.
4. **Truth is retroactive and circulatory.** The myth becomes true by being *operated,
   circulated, varied, and enacted* (extractable tokens, rituals, Mid-Autumn hooks, fan
   performance), not by being believed. "Positive unbelief" is the right register — also the
   register parafiction needs in the gallery.
5. **One world, graded canon, two registers.** Anchor Yue in the genuinely accretive Chinese
   tradition (you have the rare gift that *Wukong and the moon-rabbit already share Chapter 95*);
   expand via additive, cadence-controlled chunks; manage it with tiered canon + one curated
   database; and let an articulated thesis ("The Possibility of Fiction," a Superflat-style
   manifesto) license the commercial comic and the gallery sculptures to be the *same*
   mythology in two voices.
