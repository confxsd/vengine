## The core thesis

For a creator-owned IP, **the audience is the asset and the email list is the deed.** Social
platforms (Instagram/TikTok) are *rented* reach — great for discovery, terrible as a foundation.
The entire business is a funnel that converts rented attention into **owned** attention (email),
then converts owned attention into **money** (memberships, books, prints, editions, licensing).
The myth is what makes that attention *durable* — people don't subscribe to a feed of pictures,
they enlist in a world.

The asymmetry that makes this viable: **the same generated frames are the raw material for every
revenue stream** — a Reel today, a page in the printed book next year, an art print, the texture
on a collectible. Produce once, repackage many times.

## 1. Platform fees (verified 2025–2026)

The take-rate landscape, fact-checked against primary sources. **All platform cuts are *on top*
of payment-processor fees (~2.9% + $0.30 Stripe/PayPal) unless the platform is Merchant of
Record (MoR), which folds processing + global sales-tax/VAT handling into its fee.**

| Channel | Platform cut | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Patreon** | **10%** standard (new creators after Aug 4 2025) | old 8%/12% tiers consolidated; pre-Aug-4 creators grandfathered (lost if they unpublish+republish) |
| **Ko-fi** | **0%** tips · **5%** shop/memberships/commissions (free plan); **Ko-fi Gold $12/mo** removes the 5% | processor fees still apply on both |
| **Substack** | **10%** of paid subs; **$5/mo min** ($30/yr) | + Stripe → effective ~13–16%; ~3% free→paid is a realistic median |
| **Gumroad** | **10% + $0.50** direct (own site) · **30%** marketplace | **MoR since Jan 1 2025** (handles global tax) |
| **Lemon Squeezy** | **5% + $0.50** (floor; +intl/PayPal/sub surcharges) | **MoR**; acquired by Stripe Jul 2024 |
| **Webtoon Canvas** | **50% net ad share**; qualify at **>1,000 subs AND >40,000 monthly pageviews** | per-1k-pageview $ is anecdotal (~$0.28–0.50, unverified) |

**Webtoon earnings reality (from Webtoon's 2024 SEC S-1, via consistent secondary reporting):**
average **professional** creator ~**$48,000/yr**; **top 100 ~$1M each**; 483 creators earned
$100k+ in 2023. "Professional" is a defined subset — *not* the Canvas/amateur long tail, which
mostly earns little. Treat Webtoon as a **discovery + credibility** surface, not a primary income
plan.

**Implication:** for selling your *own* products, **own-site + a MoR checkout (Gumroad direct or
Lemon Squeezy)** keeps the most margin and removes the tax-compliance burden. Reserve
marketplaces (Webtoon, Gumroad discovery) for *reach*, not *margin*.

## 2. The funnel (rented reach → owned audience → revenue)

```
  Instagram Reels / carousels  ──►  link-in-bio / lead magnet  ──►  EMAIL LIST  ──►  paid
   (discovery, rented)              (the conversion moment)        (owned, durable)   (money)
        TikTok / Threads                                                │
        Webtoon Canvas (credibility)                                    ├─ memberships (Patreon/Ko-fi/Substack)
                                                                        ├─ books & art books (crowdfunded → POD/print run)
                                                                        ├─ prints & merch (own store, POD)
                                                                        └─ editions & licensing (art-world / IP)
```

- **Top of funnel — discovery.** Reels and carousels are the cheapest reach for art/comic
  accounts. Cadence and format matter more than polish; consistency compounds. *(Exact 2026 IG
  format/cadence benchmarks are in the pending production/publishing research stream.)*
- **The conversion moment — a lead magnet.** Don't just drop a "follow me" link. Offer something
  the world-fan wants: a free **"codex" / first chapter PDF**, a wallpaper set of Yue, a
  "fragment of the lost archive." This is where the myth's *extractable tokens* (Jenkins) do
  commercial work. Tooling: **Beehiiv** or **Substack** for the list; **ConvertKit/Kit** if you
  want automation.
- **Owned audience — email.** The list is the only channel you own outright. It is the launch
  pad for every crowdfunder and product drop. Grow it deliberately from day one, even at zero
  audience — 200 true fans on a list beats 20k passive followers.
- **Monetization — sequence it (see §5).**

**Conversion benchmarks (rules of thumb, confirm in the live pass):** social→email capture of
1–3% of engaged viewers is healthy; email→paid of ~1–5% per launch; ~3% free→paid for ongoing
memberships. Small numbers — which is why **list size × myth-driven loyalty × repackaged
catalog** is the equation, not any single conversion rate.

## 3. Owned storefront & fulfillment

- **Digital + simple physical, own site:** **Gumroad (direct, MoR)** or **Lemon Squeezy (MoR)**
  — lowest setup friction, no tax headaches, good for PDFs, wallpaper packs, digital chapters.
- **Full-catalog store / brand:** **Shopify** when the catalog and merch justify a monthly fee
  and you want full brand control + POD app ecosystem. **Big Cartel** is a cheaper indie middle
  ground.
- **Print-on-demand:**
  - *Merch / art prints:* Printful / Printify / Gelato (Gelato has strong global/local print
    network) — no inventory, lower margin, fine to start.
  - *Comic & art books:* POD quality has improved but **for the hero artifact (the long-form
    book) a crowdfunded short print run beats POD** on unit economics and object quality. POD is
    the safety net / backlist; the print run is the event.
- **Editions / sculptures (art-world tier):** fulfilled as *limited editions* (small numbered
  runs), priced as art objects not merch, sold direct (own site / mailing list) and/or via
  gallery. See §4.

## 4. Premium / art-world & the collectible-character economy

The myth's gallery register is also a revenue register, and a high-margin one.

- **Limited-edition physical objects.** Sculpture editions, art books, and prints are priced as
  *editioned art*, not merch: scarcity + numbering + artist context drive price. Sold direct to
  the list or through a gallery (gallery typically ~50%, but adds legitimacy + collectors).
- **The art-toy / designer-collectible market is the bridge between comic and gallery.**
  Character-driven collectibles are a large, fast-growing category — **Pop Mart / Labubu, KAWS,
  Bearbrick, Yoshitomo Nara** show that *a single strong character* can sustain a collectibles
  business with blind-box, drop, and limited-edition mechanics. A Yue figure is the obvious
  first object: small, characterful, repeatable, and it *circulates the myth* (a carrier, in
  hyperstition terms) while it sells. *(Market-size figures and Pop Mart benchmarks are in the
  pending research stream.)*
- **Why this matters strategically:** the same IP that earns $5 on a Reel-driven print earns
  $500 on an editioned sculpture and could earn $50k on a licensing deal. The art-world register
  isn't a vanity side-quest — it's the **top of the value ladder** and the thing that makes the
  whole project read as *serious*, which in turn protects pricing everywhere below it (the
  Murakami/Superflat lesson from [MYTH-IP-STRATEGY](./MYTH-IP-STRATEGY.md) Part 3).

## 5. Sequencing for a creator with near-zero audience today

Don't build the store before the audience or the long-form book before the world is legible.
Rough order:

1. **Lock the world + the cast (now).** A short bible (Obsidian), Yue's design, the visual
   style, 3–5 signature motifs. The engine already makes the cast *visually* consistent; make it
   *narratively* consistent too.
2. **Publish consistently on Reels (months 1–6).** 4-frame vertical art comics + a recurring
   visual signature. Goal: not sales — **a feed that proves the world exists** and a trickle into
   the email list. Start the list on day one with a lead magnet ("The First Fragment").
3. **Stand up a minimal owned web page (months 2–4).** A reader for the strips + email capture +
   an "about the myth" page (the manifesto). This is the "dedicated web page" stage; it can be
   static + generated from the same frames. *(vengine has no publisher yet — greenfield; see
   PRODUCT-STATE gap #13.)*
4. **First low-risk monetization (months 4–8).** Digital first (Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy): a
   chapter PDF, a wallpaper/codex pack, then **prints** (POD). Optionally open a **Ko-fi/Patreon**
   for early believers (Ko-fi's 0% tips is the lowest-friction start).
5. **The first event — a crowdfunded book or a first edition (months 8–18).** Once the list can
   support it, run a **Kickstarter/Backerkit** for the long-form comic or the first art book.
   Crowdfunding *is* the validation, the pre-sale, and the print-run financing in one.
6. **Editions & the art-world register (12+ months, opportunistic).** First Yue
   sculpture/edition; approach the contemporary-art frame ("The Possibility of Fiction") when
   there's a body of work and a thesis to exhibit. Don't force it early.
7. **Licensing & franchise (long game).** Retain rights; let additive extensions (animation,
   games, toys) come to a proven world. Indie precedents (*Hilda*, *Lore Olympus*) scaled from
   exactly this base.

**What to avoid prematurely:** a big Shopify catalog before there's demand; a print run before a
list; an NFT/token play (reputational risk, distracts from the serious-art positioning); spreading
across five platforms before one feed is consistent; building vengine's store/publisher before
the audience proves the need.

## How this points back at the product

The business plan turns the [PRODUCT-STATE](./PRODUCT-STATE.md) gap list into priorities with a
revenue rationale:
- **Lettering + Reels/IG export** → the top-of-funnel engine. Build first.
- **Cheap reference model (Seedream v4 edit)** → makes daily posting economically sane.
- **Page/strip + PDF/print export** → unlocks the lead-magnet PDF *and* the crowdfunded book.
- **A public reader-site generator** → the "dedicated web page" stage, from the same frames.
- **Library/cast pages + canon tooling** → protect the IP's coherence over the years it compounds.
